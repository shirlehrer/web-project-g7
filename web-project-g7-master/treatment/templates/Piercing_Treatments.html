How to treat your new Piercing ?

  • DO NOT TOUCH YOUR PIERCING.
    The only time you will touch your piercing is when you are cleaning it, and you will first wash your hands.
  • DO NOT REMOVE YOUR JEWELRY.
    New piercings can shrink the moment jewelry is removed, making it painful or impossible to get it back in.

    • BODY PIERCING AFTERCARE

    USE WARM SEA SALT WATER (SALINE) SOAKS – MORNING AND EVENING
    Soaking your piercing with a warm, mild sea salt water solution will not only feel good, it will also help prevent infection, reduce the risk of scarring, and speed the healing of your piercing. Do not touch your piercing without first washing your hands; and leave your jewelry in at all times!

  • Wash your hands thoroughly.
  • Use the ratios below to create your own saline mixture:
    1. 1⁄4 teaspoon to 8 ounces of water
    2. 1/2 teaspoon to 16 ounces of water
    3. 1 teaspoon to 1 quart of water
    4. 4 teaspoons to 1 gallon of water
  • Warm the solution in the microwave, then attempt to completely submerge the piercing in the solution for 10 minutes. If the piercing is in an area that can’t be submerged, you can soak a non-woven gauze and apply that to the piercing, keeping it saturated for 10 minutes.
  • Rinse the area with clean, running water, then pat it dry with a clean paper towel.
  • If your jewelry is threaded (barbells, etc.) check it to make sure it’s still tight! Remember “righty tighty, lefty loosey.
